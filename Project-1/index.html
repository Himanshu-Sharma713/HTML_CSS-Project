A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 - 27 July 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist who served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007. Born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he studied physics and aerospace engineering. Over the next four decades, he worked as a scientist and administrator, primarily at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), playing a key role in India's civilian space programme and military missile development. Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology, Dr. Kalam also played a crucial role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.
